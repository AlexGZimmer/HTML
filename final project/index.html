Vinyl Records

The Vinyl Record

Vinyl Records began their history with the invention of the gramophone by Emile Berliner that ended up replacing the phonograph. The early 1900s would see the use of shellac in the production of records and a standardization of 78 rpm. 1948 would end up seeing the creation of vinyl records as we know them with the creation of the long-playing record or better known as a LP record. Would remain the dominant format for music till the 80's when it got supplanted by the cassette. Records would however see a comeback in the late 2000s where it has maintained its resurgence since.

The Vinyl Record Player

The first record player was released in 1895 and was a gramaphone record player. While Radio would end up putting a dent in record player sells it, it would not end it. Record players would continue selling throughout the the early and mid 20th century. However, it is in the 60s and 70s that they hit their peek and popularity and while the popularity has gone down, records players like lp's have seen a resurgence since the late 2000s.
