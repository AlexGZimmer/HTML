Cassettes

The Cassette

Developed in 1962 by Dutch engineer Lou Ottens and released to the public in 1963, cassettes would end up becoming the popular music media in the 80s due its cheaper and more portable size. It owuld lose it's popularity to cd;s though in the following decade of the 90s. Like vinyl records though it will see a resurgance of interest in the 2010s. The biggest problem with the cassette and what would cause it to fall out of favor in the 90s to the compact disc is it's tendency to get easily damaged when the cassette player starts eating the tape which as a collector it happens frequently and is pretty annoying. The cassette also has poorer sound quality in comparison to the compact disc and the vinyl record.

The Cassette player

Cassette Player's history started with the creation of the cassette in 1962 by Lou Ottens though it would be revolutionized in 1979 with the introduction of the Sony Walkman which made music portable and help make the cassette the dominant music medium of the 80s. Majority of stereos even in the compact disc era would still contain a cassette player. Boomboxes were also important in popularity of cassettes as they were more portable than a stereo system but had better sound quality then a portable player. Like stereo systems, boomboxes would still retain a cassette option even in the era of the compact disc.
